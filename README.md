**PRELUDE**

📅 [26.10.2023]

**Background:**

In this hypothetical scenario, the backdrop assumes that the counteroffensive in Ukraine has culminated with no tangible results. Putin and his regime maintain control over all of Russia. There is no fundamental shift in strategic support for Ukraine; it receives limited assistance with significant delays. These resources are enough to compensate for losses but insufficient to alter the frontline situation. Some countries, however, begin to exhibit anti-Ukraine stances.

**PEACE AGREEMENT:**

1. Ceasefire is declared.
2. New state borders along the Line of Contact (LoC) are established, with an abandonment of further territorial claims.
3. Prisoner exchange takes place.
4. Green corridors under UN protection are established for six months to facilitate population movement from occupied territories to government-controlled areas. Ukrainians from occupied regions can opt to relocate to Kyiv-controlled territories.
5. Effective security guarantees - Ukraine joins NATO and may host U.S. nuclear weapons on its soil.
6. Sanctions remain in place for Russia as retribution for past actions.

**REACTIONS:**

**Ukraine:**
- Unyielding stance: Rejects any peace agreement or negotiations, insisting on maintaining sovereign territory.

**European Union:**
- Supportive of dialogue but underscores that only just agreements can ensure lasting peace in Europe. Advocates for Ukraine's right to decide.

**China/Iran/North Korea:**
- Emphasizes the importance of seizing the opportunity for a peaceful resolution.

**Russia:**
- Privately agrees in principle but publicly seeks to include sanctions removal and territorial concessions. Pushes for a say in Ukraine's weaponry deployment and demands symbolic gestures, such as denazification.

**United States:**
- Response hinges on the administration in the White House. Hawks may back Ukraine for strategic reasons, while doves may prioritize ending the human suffering and economic repercussions.

**Global South and Neutral Players, Trumpists, Right Populists in EU:**
- Advocate for peace talks, asserting that Ukraine should seek a negotiated settlement to end the war.

**RESPONSE STRATEGIES:**

1. **Doubts About Trusting Russia:**
   - Counterargument: Ukraine is under the NATO nuclear umbrella and has separate defense pacts with the USA, UK, and others.

2. **Resisting Imperialism and Dictatorships:**
   - Counterargument: Maintain sanctions on Russia, and allocate frozen funds of $300 billion to Ukraine.

3. **Preserving Internationally Recognized Borders:**
   - Counterargument: Encourage Ukraine to become a new South Korea and invest massively in its economy after a peace deal. Highlight the risks of investing in a warzone.

4. **Protecting Ukrainians in Occupied Regions:**
   - Counterargument: Establish safe passages under EU peace forces/UN/NATO supervision, allowing people to choose their place of residence.

**PACIFIST ARGUMENTS:**

1. War's Human Toll: Ending the conflict quickly saves lives and is morally right.
2. Economic Impact: The war disrupts trade, raises energy prices, and hampers world trade.
3. No Viable Alternative: Ukraine lacks the resources for a solo fight, and Western powers are reluctant to risk nuclear escalation.
4. Wolves Are Fed and Sheep Are Safe: Ukraine secures NATO membership and investments, while Russia gains recognized territories and increased population.
